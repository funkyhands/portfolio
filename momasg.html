MoMAsg

Architecture

MoMASG celebrates Singapore's transformation from a fishing village to a global city. Its design features strategic openings to reintroduce framed views of key landmarks along the Singapore River. These openings guide visitors through the museum, offering diverse perspectives that highlight Singapore's evolution.

The architecture weaves historical landmarks as part of the exhibit, allowing the building itself to tell the story of the city's past while actively connecting visitors with the ongoing growth of Singapore. The layered facade wraps around a central void, creating a spiralling circulation that ascends through the museum's programme — from public ground-floor galleries up to more intimate exhibition spaces above.

Sited along the Singapore River near Anderson Bridge, the massing responds to the surrounding civic district context. The building's orientation frames key sightlines towards the Fullerton Hotel, the Padang, and the Marina Bay skyline, turning the city itself into an extension of the museum's narrative.
